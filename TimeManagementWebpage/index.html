SMART Goals

Specific

A goal needs to be specific. Among other reasons, it allows you to track your goal better and avoid future confusion. You should at least ask yourself the following questions:

  • What do I want to achieve?
  • Why is this goal important?
  • Who is participating?
  • Where is it located?
  • Which resources or limits are involved?
Measurable

A goal also needs to be measurable both by definition and from an evaluation perspective to track your progress. Ask yourself these questions:

  • How much?
  • How many?
  • How will I know if I am on track?
  • How will I know if I have accomplished my goals?
Achievable

Your goal should be able to answer these questions:

  • Can you afford this?
  • Do you have the skills needed?
  • Do you have the resources required?

Tip: Your goal should not be dependent upon someone else. It should be entirely focused on your own abilities and power.

Realistic

Does your goal fit? Is it you? Don't overlook these important qualities of a realistic goal. Your goal should have all of these traits:

  • worthwhile
  • cost:benefit approved
  • matches personality
  • currently desirable in socio-economic environment
  • right goal for the right moment
Time-bound

Your goal needs a deadline. This helps you create time-oriented mini-goals. Your goal should answer these questions:

  • When?
  • What should I do now?
